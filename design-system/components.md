# Components Doctrine

This document defines the reusable modular experience components for an AI-native hospitality and experience product operating system. It is an internal systems doctrine for teams and agents designing premium hospitality, entertainment, booking, dining, wellness, and package merchandising experiences.

The component system is inspired by modern luxury hospitality platforms and destination resorts. It should borrow their confidence, pacing, polish, and ability to make many experiences feel connected, but it should avoid casino-style clutter, aggressive conversion pressure, and decorative excess. The goal is a calm, cinematic, measurable system that can tell a story, support a decision, and improve through telemetry.

Components in this system are not isolated UI widgets. They are reusable experience modules: each one has a narrative role, a conversion purpose, an operational context, and a measurement contract. A component should help the guest understand the value of an experience while helping product, marketing, revenue, operations, and AI agents understand how the module should be reused, adapted, tested, and improved.

## 1. Component System Philosophy

The component system exists to make premium experience design repeatable without making it generic. It should help teams compose guest journeys from reusable modules that preserve atmosphere, commercial clarity, operational feasibility, and analytics readiness.

Core philosophy:

- **Components are story units:** Every component should answer where the guest is in the journey, what they need to feel, what they need to understand, and what action should naturally become available.
- **Luxury is expressed through restraint:** A premium component earns attention through hierarchy, imagery, timing, language, and proportion. It should not rely on visual noise, competing offers, or forced urgency.
- **Modularity should preserve continuity:** Components may be rearranged, personalized, or generated by AI, but they must still feel like one coherent experience rather than disconnected promotional blocks.
- **Conversion is a service moment:** The system should treat calls to action as useful guidance. A booking, package selection, itinerary save, inquiry, or reservation should feel timely and reversible.
- **Telemetry is part of the component contract:** Reusable modules should define what behavior matters, what signals are meaningful, and how those signals should inform future design decisions.
- **AI should generate from structure:** AI-assisted workflows should produce components with explicit purpose, audience fit, emotional tone, hierarchy, measurement intent, and operational constraints.

When a component feels impressive but does not clarify the journey, support a decision, or produce useful learning, it should be simplified.

## 2. Experience Architecture Principles

Experience architecture defines how components sequence into a complete journey. A premium hospitality interface should feel paced: arrival, orientation, desire, comparison, decision, confirmation, and continuation.

Principles:

- **Arrival before action:** Begin with a clear sense of place, occasion, and promise before asking for commitment.
- **Discovery before comparison:** Let guests understand why an experience matters before placing it beside alternatives.
- **Value before price pressure:** Show inclusions, timing, fit, and emotional payoff before emphasizing rate or savings.
- **Context before personalization:** Personal recommendations should reference the guest's likely intent, trip type, occasion, or prior behavior.
- **Decision support before checkout:** Use comparison, availability, social proof, itinerary framing, and summaries to reduce uncertainty.
- **Operational truth before launch:** Components should never promise what the property, venue, restaurant, spa, or event team cannot fulfill.
- **Measurement before optimization:** Do not experiment on a component until its intended behavior and success signals are defined.

Recommended narrative sequence:

1. **Set the scene:** Establish destination, mood, occasion, and primary promise.
2. **Reveal the experience set:** Present a curated range of stays, events, dining, wellness, and packages.
3. **Deepen desire:** Use editorial, imagery, artist or chef context, timelines, inclusions, and atmosphere.
4. **Clarify fit:** Help guests compare experiences by occasion, schedule, budget, group size, and energy level.
5. **Offer a natural next step:** Present booking, saving, inquiry, reservation, or package selection at the moment intent is strongest.
6. **Preserve continuity:** After action, confirm the choice and suggest relevant additions without breaking trust.

## 3. Component Classification Framework

Each component should be classified by role, not by surface appearance. This allows AI agents and product teams to select the right module based on journey intent.

Primary component classes:

- **Narrative components:** Establish atmosphere, context, editorial meaning, and emotional momentum.
- **Conversion components:** Help guests commit, reserve, book, compare, save, inquire, or continue.
- **Package components:** Translate bundles, offers, inclusions, and multi-part experiences into clear value.
- **Hospitality components:** Support stays, rooms, property details, service expectations, wellness, dining, and guest care.
- **Entertainment components:** Present artists, events, lineups, venues, schedules, and entertainment-led trips.
- **Editorial components:** Provide story, curation, recommendations, social proof, seasonal context, and taste.
- **Navigation components:** Help guests move through complex experience inventories without feeling lost.
- **Mobile components:** Convert broad journeys into focused, thumb-friendly, sequential surfaces.
- **Persistent components:** Keep high-intent actions available without overwhelming exploration.
- **Analytics-aware components:** Expose measurable states, user signals, and experiment hooks while remaining visually calm.
- **Adaptive components:** Future modules that respond to guest context, inventory, behavior, seasonality, and operational constraints.

Every component should carry this minimum metadata:

- **Journey stage:** arrival, discovery, evaluation, decision, confirmation, continuation, recovery.
- **Experience domain:** stay, event, dining, wellness, package, property, itinerary, membership, offer.
- **Primary emotion:** anticipation, confidence, indulgence, relief, curiosity, trust, belonging, celebration.
- **Primary action:** explore, compare, save, reserve, book, inquire, upgrade, customize, share, continue.
- **Telemetry intent:** awareness, engagement, preference, intent, conversion, retention, operational readiness.
- **AI generation mode:** compose, summarize, personalize, compare, variant, translate, operationalize.

## 4. Narrative Flow Components

Narrative flow components create cinematic pacing. They should turn a collection of offers into a meaningful journey.

### Experience Hero

- **Purpose:** Establish the primary destination, campaign, package, property, or experience promise.
- **Emotional role:** Create arrival, anticipation, and trust. The guest should feel they have entered a composed premium environment.
- **UX intent:** Make the central experience legible in the first viewport while hinting at the next section.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Highest narrative priority. It should include one dominant message, one primary action, and one secondary path.
- **Conversion role:** Begin intent capture through booking, package exploration, saved itinerary, or availability search without forcing premature checkout.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track hero impression, media engagement, primary CTA selection, secondary CTA selection, scroll depth past hero, and availability search initiation.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate one clear promise, a specific audience or occasion, concise supporting copy, and CTA language tied to the next journey step. Avoid generic luxury adjectives.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop may use immersive imagery with layered content. Mobile should prioritize readable copy, image quality, and a reachable action area.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Treat the hero as an experience entry point, not an advertisement. It should establish context and direction with disciplined restraint.

### Featured Experience Rail

- **Purpose:** Surface a curated set of high-value experiences across stays, events, dining, wellness, or packages.
- **Emotional role:** Create discovery and possibility without overwhelming the guest.
- **UX intent:** Let users scan a small number of strong options and move into detail.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Secondary to the hero, often the first breadth module after arrival.
- **Conversion role:** Moves users from passive inspiration to active selection.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track rail impression, card visibility, card selection, save actions, filter context, and position-based engagement.
- **AI generation guidance:** Curate by occasion, season, audience, or intent. Explain why each experience belongs in the set.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop can show horizontal rails or editorial grids. Mobile should use swipeable cards with stable card dimensions and clear tap targets.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Curation should feel intentional. Do not turn the rail into a dumping ground for inventory.

### Experience Timeline

- **Purpose:** Explain the sequence of a stay, event weekend, dining journey, wellness day, or multi-part package.
- **Emotional role:** Reduce uncertainty and help the guest imagine themselves inside the experience.
- **UX intent:** Convert abstract value into a believable itinerary.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Works best after desire has been established and before final decision.
- **Conversion role:** Supports commitment by clarifying flow, timing, inclusions, and effort required.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track expansion of timeline steps, dwell time by step, package CTA after timeline, and drop-off around operational constraints.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate steps around guest moments, not internal operations. Include arrival, highlight, transition, and completion states.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop may present a horizontal or vertical timeline. Mobile should use stacked sequential cards with concise summaries.
- **Implementation philosophy:** The timeline should make the experience feel more real, not more complicated.

### Seasonal Campaign Module

- **Purpose:** Frame experiences around a season, holiday, artist residency, culinary festival, wellness reset, or destination moment.
- **Emotional role:** Create timeliness and relevance without manipulative urgency.
- **UX intent:** Connect multiple components under one campaign story.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Can lead a page or anchor a mid-page campaign band.
- **Conversion role:** Directs guests toward seasonally relevant packages, events, and reservations.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track campaign impression, offer selection by segment, seasonal CTA conversion, date-range interactions, and return visits.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate from a specific calendar context, audience, and operational availability. Avoid fake scarcity.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should compress to one campaign promise, a small set of featured items, and one action.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Seasonality should provide coherence, not excuse visual overload.

## 5. Conversion Components

Conversion components help guests act with confidence. They should be useful, contextual, and calm.

### Immersive CTA Section

- **Purpose:** Provide a high-confidence transition from inspiration to action.
- **Emotional role:** Make the next step feel natural, rewarding, and low-friction.
- **UX intent:** Focus attention on one primary action after the guest has received enough context.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Should appear at key narrative thresholds, not after every module.
- **Conversion role:** Drives booking, availability search, reservation, inquiry, itinerary save, or package selection.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track CTA impression, action type, prior content exposure, conversion path, hesitation signals, and downstream completion.
- **AI generation guidance:** Match CTA copy to user readiness. Prefer "Check availability," "Build your weekend," or "Reserve this experience" over pressure language.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop can use a full-width band. Mobile should keep copy short and action reachable.
- **Implementation philosophy:** A CTA should feel like concierge guidance at the right moment.

### Premium Offer Stack

- **Purpose:** Present a focused set of high-value offers with clear tradeoffs.
- **Emotional role:** Create confidence and a feeling of curated access.
- **UX intent:** Help guests understand which offer fits their occasion.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Use when multiple offers compete for the same intent.
- **Conversion role:** Supports selection, upgrade, and qualified package conversion.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track offer impressions, comparison behavior, selected offer, abandoned offers, and upgrade path.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate offer labels by guest outcome, not internal rate code. Include value rationale and fit criteria.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop may support side-by-side comparison. Mobile should use stacked offer summaries with expandable details.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Premium offers should be few, legible, and differentiated.

### Experience Comparison Module

- **Purpose:** Help guests compare rooms, packages, properties, restaurants, wellness services, or event options.
- **Emotional role:** Reduce decision anxiety.
- **UX intent:** Make meaningful differences easy to understand.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Appears after users have shown evaluation intent.
- **Conversion role:** Increases confidence before booking or inquiry.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track compared items, attributes viewed, sort/filter use, selection after comparison, and unresolved exits.
- **AI generation guidance:** Summarize differences by guest need: best for celebration, quiet retreat, family weekend, nightlife, culinary focus, or wellness reset.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop may use comparison tables or cards. Mobile should use two-item comparisons, sticky attribute labels, or guided comparison steps.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Compare what matters to the guest, not every available attribute.

### Social Proof Layer

- **Purpose:** Provide trust signals through press, ratings, guest sentiment, awards, partner credibility, or popularity indicators.
- **Emotional role:** Validate desire without making the experience feel mass-market.
- **UX intent:** Reinforce confidence near decision points.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Supportive, never dominant.
- **Conversion role:** Helps guests act when they are interested but uncertain.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track proof exposure before conversion, proof type engagement, sentiment expansion, and segment-specific lift.
- **AI generation guidance:** Use specific, sourced signals. Summarize sentiment accurately and avoid inflated claims.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should show one or two proof points with expansion for detail.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Social proof should be quiet, credible, and contextually placed.

### Testimonial Module

- **Purpose:** Show human experience quality through selected guest, artist, chef, host, or partner perspectives.
- **Emotional role:** Build trust, warmth, and specificity.
- **UX intent:** Help users understand what the experience feels like from a credible viewpoint.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Works best after an experience has been introduced.
- **Conversion role:** Supports emotional commitment and reduces perceived risk.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track testimonial exposure, expansion, source type, related CTA clicks, and assisted conversion.
- **AI generation guidance:** AI may summarize approved testimonials but should not fabricate quotes or identities.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop can support quote plus image or context. Mobile should prioritize readable quote length and source credibility.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Testimonials should be evidence, not decoration.

## 6. Package Components

Package components turn complex hospitality value into understandable, bookable, measurable offers.

### Weekend Package Module

- **Purpose:** Present a complete weekend experience with stay, dining, entertainment, wellness, and optional upgrades.
- **Emotional role:** Help guests imagine an effortless premium escape.
- **UX intent:** Translate a multi-part itinerary into a clear package promise.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Often serves as a central merchandising module for campaigns and landing pages.
- **Conversion role:** Drives package selection, availability search, and itinerary customization.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track package impression, inclusion expansion, date selection, upgrade interest, save actions, and booking completion.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate by occasion, audience, dates, inclusions, operational constraints, and revenue priorities. Include why the bundle belongs together.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should present the package as a guided sequence: promise, inclusions, dates, starting price, action.
- **Implementation philosophy:** A package should feel curated, not bundled for convenience alone.

### Stay + Event Bundle

- **Purpose:** Connect room inventory with event attendance, venue timing, dining, and transportation context.
- **Emotional role:** Reduce planning friction and increase excitement around a destination event.
- **UX intent:** Show the advantage of booking the stay and event as one coordinated experience.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Best used around concerts, residencies, sporting events, festivals, and limited-run entertainment.
- **Conversion role:** Drives bundled conversion, room upgrades, event add-ons, and pre-arrival planning.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track event selection, room pairing, date alignment, bundle completion, add-on selection, and sellout recovery paths.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate bundles from verified event schedules, room availability, audience segment, and arrival/departure logic.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should emphasize event date, room fit, included access, and a persistent booking action.
- **Implementation philosophy:** The bundle should solve coordination, not simply cross-sell.

### Curated Recommendation Rail

- **Purpose:** Recommend relevant packages, dining, wellness, events, or upgrades based on context.
- **Emotional role:** Feel like a knowledgeable host offering helpful next steps.
- **UX intent:** Continue discovery without forcing the user back into broad browsing.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Usually appears after a detail page, booking step, or saved item.
- **Conversion role:** Drives secondary conversion, itinerary building, and qualified upsell.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track recommendation basis, impression, acceptance, dismissal, save, and downstream attachment rate.
- **AI generation guidance:** State the recommendation logic internally. Use guest-facing language that feels natural, not algorithmic.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should limit recommendations to a small, highly relevant set.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Recommendations must be explainable and useful. Relevance matters more than volume.

## 7. Hospitality Components

Hospitality components make stays, service, property value, wellness, dining, and care legible.

### Dining Experience Feature

- **Purpose:** Present a restaurant, tasting menu, chef collaboration, bar program, or culinary package.
- **Emotional role:** Create appetite, occasion, and trust in the quality of the experience.
- **UX intent:** Help guests understand cuisine, setting, signature moments, availability, and reservation path.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Can stand alone as a feature or support a larger itinerary.
- **Conversion role:** Drives reservation, package attachment, waitlist, or saved itinerary.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track menu interest, date/time selection, reservation CTA, image engagement, and package attachment.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate specific culinary language grounded in cuisine, setting, chef intent, and guest occasion. Avoid empty indulgence copy.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should prioritize imagery, cuisine summary, hours, location, and reservation action.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Dining should feel sensorial and practical at the same time.

### Property Comparison Module

- **Purpose:** Help guests compare towers, room classes, resorts, venues, or property zones.
- **Emotional role:** Create orientation and confidence in choosing the right setting.
- **UX intent:** Make location, atmosphere, amenities, price position, and occasion fit clear.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Useful during booking evaluation and destination planning.
- **Conversion role:** Reduces abandonment caused by uncertainty across property options.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track compared properties, selected criteria, map interactions, room-path entry, and unresolved comparison exits.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate comparison summaries by guest need and trip type. Do not overstate differences beyond verified facts.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should use guided comparison, maps, or expandable attribute groups rather than dense tables.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Property comparison is service design. It should help guests choose where they belong.

### Wellness Experience Feature

- **Purpose:** Present spa, recovery, fitness, pool, mindfulness, or wellness programming.
- **Emotional role:** Create calm, restoration, and personal relevance.
- **UX intent:** Explain treatment benefits, timing, setting, preparation, and booking path.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Can be a feature component or an itinerary enhancement.
- **Conversion role:** Drives appointment booking, package attachment, and pre-arrival planning.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track treatment exploration, duration selection, add-on interest, appointment search, and attachment to stay.
- **AI generation guidance:** Use grounded wellness language. Avoid medical claims unless approved and verified.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should support quick comparison by duration, benefit, and availability.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Wellness components should feel soothing but operationally precise.

## 8. Entertainment Components

Entertainment components support artist-led, venue-led, and event-led destination journeys.

### Artist Spotlight

- **Purpose:** Feature an artist, performer, residency, chef, host, or cultural collaborator.
- **Emotional role:** Create emotional connection, credibility, and anticipation.
- **UX intent:** Explain why the person matters and how the guest can experience their work.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** High narrative weight for entertainment-led campaigns.
- **Conversion role:** Drives event ticketing, stay bundles, reminders, and content engagement.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track media plays, bio expansion, show date interaction, bundle selection, and reminder signups.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate concise, specific context based on approved bios, event facts, and audience relevance. Do not invent credentials.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should pair a strong image or media moment with immediate event dates and actions.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Spotlight the person as part of the destination story, not as a detached profile.

### Event Lineup Grid

- **Purpose:** Present multiple events, performances, shows, or activations in a scannable structure.
- **Emotional role:** Create energy, range, and a sense of momentum.
- **UX intent:** Help users discover dates, genres, venues, and fit quickly.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Broad inventory module that should still feel curated.
- **Conversion role:** Drives event detail views, ticketing, bundle entry, reminders, and saved itinerary actions.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track filters, genre selection, date selection, venue selection, card engagement, and no-result states.
- **AI generation guidance:** Group events by meaningful guest intent such as weekend, genre, celebration, late night, family-friendly, or premium access.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop can support filters and dense grids. Mobile should use chips, date grouping, and compact cards.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Lineups should feel editorially organized, not like raw inventory.

### Venue Moment Module

- **Purpose:** Explain the atmosphere, capacity, access, seating, and experiential value of a venue.
- **Emotional role:** Help guests picture the night and trust logistics.
- **UX intent:** Bridge entertainment desire with practical planning.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Supports event detail pages and bundles.
- **Conversion role:** Drives seat selection, premium access, arrival planning, and package attachment.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track seating map engagement, premium area interest, venue detail expansion, and access-related questions.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate venue descriptions from verified facts, guest flow, sightlines, amenities, and access options.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should prioritize venue location, arrival timing, seating/action path, and accessibility details.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Venue context should remove friction while preserving excitement.

## 9. Editorial Components

Editorial components provide taste, context, and guided discovery.

### Editorial Split Layout

- **Purpose:** Pair story and media with a focused experience, package, restaurant, room, or event.
- **Emotional role:** Create intimacy and editorial confidence.
- **UX intent:** Let content breathe while connecting it to action.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Medium to high narrative weight depending on placement.
- **Conversion role:** Converts through education and desire rather than pressure.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track section view, media engagement, text expansion, CTA selection, and assisted conversion.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate one editorial angle, one specific proof point, and one natural next step.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop can use asymmetry. Mobile should stack media, headline, concise copy, and action.
- **Implementation philosophy:** The layout should feel like a composed story page, not a card inside another card.

### Guide / Itinerary Module

- **Purpose:** Present a recommended path through a day, weekend, property, or campaign.
- **Emotional role:** Create reassurance and the feeling of expert curation.
- **UX intent:** Help users move from inspiration to a plan.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Works well after broad discovery or before package selection.
- **Conversion role:** Drives saved itineraries, bundled booking, reservations, and share actions.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track itinerary saves, step expansion, component-to-booking path, add/remove actions, and share events.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate by guest type, occasion, schedule, energy level, and inventory. Explain assumptions for internal review.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should support sequential progression and quick saves.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Guides should make planning feel effortless and editable.

### Content Proof Module

- **Purpose:** Support claims with editorial context, awards, press mentions, chef notes, artist notes, or behind-the-scenes detail.
- **Emotional role:** Add depth and credibility.
- **UX intent:** Give interested users more substance without slowing the main path.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Supportive, progressive disclosure.
- **Conversion role:** Assists high-consideration decisions and premium upgrade confidence.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track proof expansion, source engagement, related action, and segment-specific influence.
- **AI generation guidance:** Summarize accurately and cite approved source context internally.
- **Responsive behavior:** Mobile should use concise expandable sections.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Proof should enrich the story while staying accountable.

## 10. Navigation Components

Navigation components help guests move through complex experience ecosystems with clarity.

Key navigation patterns:

- **Experience Category Nav:** Allows movement between stay, dine, play, wellness, events, packages, and property content. It should reflect guest intent rather than org structure.
- **Occasion Filter Bar:** Lets users browse by celebration, weekend escape, date night, business travel, family, nightlife, relaxation, or culinary discovery.
- **Date-Aware Filter:** Connects availability, events, dining, and rooms around a specific visit window.
- **Journey Breadcrumb:** Shows progress across package discovery, configuration, booking, and confirmation.
- **Contextual Back Path:** Returns users to the prior campaign, list, itinerary, or comparison state without losing filters.
- **Saved Experience Entry:** Keeps saved items, itineraries, and recently viewed experiences accessible.

Navigation rules:

- Navigation should create orientation before breadth.
- Labels should match guest mental models.
- Filters should be meaningful enough to change the experience set.
- Mobile navigation should expose the current context and primary next step before secondary destinations.
- AI-generated navigation recommendations should be based on known content structure, not invented categories.

## 11. Mobile UX Components

Mobile is the primary mode for immersive discovery, weekend planning, event browsing, reservation behavior, and on-property continuation. Components should become more sequential, focused, and reachable on mobile.

Mobile-first component patterns:

- **Mobile Conversion Dock:** A persistent bottom action area for high-intent journeys.
- **Swipeable Experience Rail:** A small curated rail with stable card dimensions and visible progress.
- **Compact Package Summary:** A collapsed package view showing promise, dates, inclusions count, price cue, and action.
- **Expandable Detail Sheet:** Progressive disclosure for inclusions, schedule, policies, venue details, and comparison attributes.
- **Itinerary Stepper:** Sequential experience planning for multi-part stays, event weekends, or wellness days.
- **Thumb-Reachable Filters:** Bottom sheets or segmented controls for date, category, occasion, and price posture.
- **Save / Continue Pattern:** A lightweight way to preserve intent when mobile sessions are interrupted.

Mobile behavior principles:

- Use one primary action per screen state.
- Keep sticky actions clear but visually quiet.
- Avoid dense comparison tables.
- Use progressive disclosure for operational details.
- Preserve emotional imagery without sacrificing legibility.
- Maintain state across interrupted sessions.

## 12. Sticky and Persistent Interaction Components

Persistent components should support continuity and action without creating pressure.

### Sticky Booking Layer

- **Purpose:** Keep booking or availability access present during high-intent exploration.
- **Emotional role:** Provide reassurance that the next step is available when the guest is ready.
- **UX intent:** Reduce friction after intent forms.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Persistent but secondary to content until the user engages.
- **Conversion role:** Drives availability search, package booking, reservation, or inquiry.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track exposure duration, expansion, collapsed state, CTA selection, scroll position at action, and booking completion.
- **AI generation guidance:** AI should recommend when a sticky layer is appropriate based on journey stage and intent signals.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop may use a side or bottom booking layer. Mobile usually uses a compact bottom dock.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Persistence should feel helpful, not like pursuit.

### Mobile Conversion Dock

- **Purpose:** Provide reachable primary action and key decision summary on mobile.
- **Emotional role:** Create confidence and continuity in a constrained viewport.
- **UX intent:** Let users act without losing context.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Always below content priority, but consistently available.
- **Conversion role:** Supports booking, reservation, package selection, ticket purchase, save, or inquiry.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track dock visibility, interaction, price/date summary exposure, disabled states, and conversion path.
- **AI generation guidance:** Generate concise dock labels based on current object and readiness state.
- **Responsive behavior:** Must respect safe areas, avoid covering critical content, and remain accessible.
- **Implementation philosophy:** The dock should summarize the decision, not repeat the whole page.

### Persistent Itinerary Tray

- **Purpose:** Keep selected rooms, events, dining, wellness, and upgrades visible during planning.
- **Emotional role:** Create control and reduce planning anxiety.
- **UX intent:** Help users understand the emerging trip or package.
- **Hierarchy behavior:** Persistent in planning flows, minimized in exploration flows.
- **Conversion role:** Drives completion of multi-part itineraries and packages.
- **Analytics considerations:** Track item additions, removals, tray opens, conflicts, completion rate, and abandoned itinerary states.
- **AI generation guidance:** AI may suggest missing itinerary pieces but should distinguish suggestions from selected items.
- **Responsive behavior:** Desktop can use a side tray. Mobile should use a bottom sheet or summary chip.
- **Implementation philosophy:** Persistence should preserve context across modules.

## 13. Analytics-Aware Components

Analytics-aware components define what the system should learn from user behavior. Telemetry should be useful, privacy-aware, and aligned with product decisions.

Component analytics contract:

- **Impression:** When the component became meaningfully visible.
- **Engagement:** What the user expanded, played, swiped, saved, filtered, compared, or selected.
- **Intent:** Which signals suggest interest, hesitation, preference, or readiness.
- **Conversion:** Which downstream action the component influenced.
- **Context:** Page, campaign, experience type, date range, audience segment, inventory state, and recommendation basis.
- **Outcome:** Booking, reservation, inquiry, itinerary save, package attachment, upgrade, waitlist, or abandonment.
- **Learning loop:** How the signal should inform content, sequencing, recommendation logic, or package strategy.

Recommended event language:

- `component_viewed`
- `component_engaged`
- `experience_saved`
- `package_compared`
- `offer_selected`
- `availability_started`
- `booking_path_entered`
- `recommendation_accepted`
- `recommendation_dismissed`
- `itinerary_item_added`
- `sticky_layer_expanded`
- `mobile_dock_cta_selected`

Analytics principles:

- Instrument only signals that can inform a decision.
- Avoid tracking that makes the interface feel surveillant or manipulative.
- Preserve content and experiment identifiers so learning survives redesigns.
- Distinguish organic discovery from AI-recommended discovery.
- Capture non-conversion signals such as saves, comparisons, returns, and dismissals.
- Use telemetry to improve relevance, not to intensify pressure.

## 14. AI-Generated Component Principles

AI agents should generate components as structured experience decisions, not as freeform UI copy.

AI generation requirements:

- **Start with intent:** Define audience, occasion, journey stage, emotional role, and primary action before generating copy or layout.
- **Use verified context:** Ground experiences in approved property, room, event, dining, wellness, inventory, and operational facts.
- **Respect hierarchy:** Generate a clear primary message, supporting details, and secondary content. Avoid equal-weight blocks.
- **Preserve brand behavior:** Use calm luxury, editorial specificity, operational clarity, and restrained conversion language.
- **Produce modular output:** Components should include reusable fields such as title, summary, media direction, inclusions, CTA, analytics notes, and responsive guidance.
- **Expose assumptions:** AI should document assumptions that affect pricing, availability, eligibility, scheduling, or fulfillment.
- **Support variants:** Generate experiment-ready alternatives with a hypothesis and success metric.
- **Keep humans accountable:** AI-generated modules should be editable, reviewable, and state-aware before publication.

AI should avoid:

- Invented scarcity, credentials, amenities, or availability.
- Generic phrases such as "unforgettable luxury awaits" without concrete experience value.
- Overstuffed modules that try to tell every story at once.
- Recommendations that cannot explain their basis.
- Copy that pressures users before they have enough context.

Reusable AI component prompt shape:

## 15. Future Adaptive Components

Future adaptive components should respond to context while preserving taste, explainability, and operational control.

Adaptive component directions:

- **Contextual Experience Hero:** Adapts hero emphasis by season, guest segment, visit date, campaign source, or inventory state.
- **Adaptive Package Builder:** Recommends room, event, dining, and wellness combinations based on occasion and availability.
- **Dynamic Recommendation Rail:** Reorders experiences based on behavior, current trip context, and business rules.
- **Inventory-Aware Offer Stack:** Adjusts offer visibility according to live availability, margin, capacity, and guest fit.
- **On-Property Continuation Layer:** Shifts from pre-arrival planning to day-of itinerary, reservations, wayfinding, and service recovery.
- **Experiment-Aware Editorial Module:** Serves approved narrative variants tied to hypotheses and measurement plans.
- **AI Concierge Summary:** Explains selected experiences, unresolved choices, and recommended next steps in a concise editable format.

Adaptive governance:

- Adaptation should improve relevance, not hide important choices.
- Guests should never be misled by personalized ordering, pricing, or availability.
- Operational teams should be able to inspect why an adaptive component appeared.
- AI-generated personalization must respect privacy, consent, and approved data use.
- Adaptive systems should preserve editorial quality even when content is assembled dynamically.

## 16. Operational Governance Principles

Component governance keeps the system coherent as it scales across teams, properties, campaigns, agents, and experiments.

Governance rules:

- **Every component has an owner:** Assign ownership for content quality, design behavior, implementation readiness, analytics, and operational accuracy.
- **Every reusable module has a purpose:** Do not add components because a page needs more content. Add them because the journey requires a specific role.
- **Every published module has a state:** Draft, review, approved, scheduled, live, paused, expired, archived.
- **Every conversion module has a measurement plan:** Define primary action, assisted action, guardrail metric, and learning objective.
- **Every AI-generated module is reviewable:** AI output should include assumptions, source facts, and editable fields.
- **Every package component maps to fulfillment:** Inclusions, availability, ownership, service handoffs, and restrictions must be clear.
- **Every experiment preserves learning:** Variants should be named, measured, summarized, and connected back to reusable guidance.
- **Every component should degrade gracefully:** Missing inventory, sold-out events, unavailable dining slots, and incomplete data should produce useful alternatives.

Operational review checklist:

- Does the component support a real journey stage?
- Is the emotional role clear?
- Is the primary action appropriate for the user's readiness?
- Are inclusions, facts, and constraints accurate?
- Is the hierarchy calm and legible on mobile?
- Are analytics events meaningful and privacy-aware?
- Can AI generate or adapt this module without inventing facts?
- Does the component connect to the surrounding story?
- Can operations fulfill what the component promises?

## Implementation Philosophy

Implementation should translate this doctrine into durable, accessible, mobile-first components without overfitting to a single page or campaign. The eventual component library may use modern frontend frameworks, design tokens, structured content, and analytics schemas, but the first implementation requirement is conceptual clarity.

Implementation principles:

- Build from reusable primitives, but document components by experience role.
- Keep content, media, actions, and telemetry as explicit fields.
- Support structured CMS or AI-generated inputs without losing editorial control.
- Preserve accessibility, responsive behavior, and performance as production requirements.
- Avoid nested-card visual systems that make every module feel equally important.
- Provide empty, loading, unavailable, sold-out, and error states for operational reality.
- Keep analytics identifiers stable across visual redesigns.
- Design components so they can participate in experiments without duplicating logic.
- Treat mobile behavior as a first-class specification, not a breakpoint afterthought.

The long-term strength of this system will come from components that can be composed into many journeys while still feeling specific to a guest, a property, a season, and a moment. A reusable component is successful when it preserves hospitality: the user feels oriented, understood, and invited toward the right next step.
