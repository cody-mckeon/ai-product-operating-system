# Entertainment Pilot Design Rationale

## Project Context

The current Entertainment experience is primarily event discovery and ticket purchase.

Current journey:

Browse Events
→ Select Event
→ Purchase Tickets

Opportunity analysis identified potential gaps in planning context, venue understanding, and entertainment discovery support.

The purpose of these explorations is not to redesign the entire experience.

The purpose is to evaluate whether contextual information can improve guest understanding and decision-making while preserving ticket purchase as the primary conversion goal.

---

## Design Principles

All explorations should:

- Keep event discovery central.
- Preserve ticket purchase as the primary CTA.
- Avoid inventing packages.
- Avoid inventing loyalty benefits.
- Avoid inventing operational promises.
- Avoid introducing unsupported business capabilities.
- Remain grounded in documented evidence.
- Support exploration rather than production implementation.

---

## Exploration Direction 1

### Concept
Event-Anchored Timeline Context

### Hypothesis

Guests may think about entertainment within the context of their visit dates.

Providing before, during, and after-event context may improve planning confidence.

### What We Are Testing

- Does date-based context improve understanding?
- Do guests engage with planning information?
- Does timeline framing support ticket decisions?

### Potential Risks

- Additional content may distract from conversion.
- Timeline structure may increase cognitive load.

---

## Exploration Direction 2

### Concept

Venue-Clustered Event Context

### Hypothesis

Guests may think about entertainment based on venue rather than artist alone.

Providing venue information and related events may improve venue understanding and discovery.

### What We Are Testing

- Does venue context increase engagement?
- Do guests interact with venue information?
- Does venue grouping support discovery?

### Potential Risks

- Venue information may compete with artist-focused discovery.
- Additional venue content may create clutter.

---

## Exploration Direction 3

### Concept

Question-Driven Planning Context

### Hypothesis

Guests often have planning questions before purchasing tickets.

Providing verified planning answers may reduce uncertainty.

### What We Are Testing

- Which planning questions matter most?
- Does FAQ content increase engagement?
- Does information reduce friction?

### Potential Risks

- Content maintenance requirements.
- Policy accuracy concerns.

---

## Exploration Direction 4

### Concept

Multi-Event Weekend View

### Hypothesis

Guests may benefit from understanding what else is happening during their visit window.

Providing adjacent-event context may increase discovery.

### What We Are Testing

- Do guests explore additional events?
- Does contextual event visibility improve engagement?
- Does adjacent-event awareness help planning?

### Potential Risks

- Additional events may distract from primary conversion.
- Discovery may become overwhelming.

---

## Exploration Summary

These concepts represent different approaches to contextualizing entertainment discovery.

The goal of this exploration is not to select a final design.

The goal is to evaluate which contextual models create the greatest guest value while preserving business goals and ticket conversion behavior.

Success will be evaluated through future design review, telemetry planning, stakeholder review, and analytics validation.
