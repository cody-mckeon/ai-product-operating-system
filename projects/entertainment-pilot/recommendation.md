# Recommendation

## Executive Summary

### Top Recommendation

Strengthen the entertainment-to-resort value connection.

### Supporting Evidence

The observed Entertainment page foregrounds event browsing, ticket/table CTAs, venue booking actions, and global room booking, but documented opportunities show limited visible connection between entertainment intent and packages, rooms, dining, loyalty, or other resort value moments. This recommendation is supported by Opportunity 1, Opportunity 5, and Opportunity 6, plus the current-state inventory of event card CTAs, global navigation, and loyalty placement.

### Why It Matters

Entertainment appears to be a high-intent discovery surface. If guests are considering a show, nightclub, dayclub, or venue visit, the business has a strategic opening to connect that intent to a broader resort journey while helping guests understand the full value of planning entertainment as part of a stay.

### Priority Order of All Recommendations

1. Strengthen the entertainment-to-resort value connection.
2. Improve date- and mobile-led entertainment discovery.
3. Increase confidence in commercial and operational decisions.
4. Connect event choices to venue and premium context.

Priority 2

### Risks and Assumptions

This direction assumes mobile visibility and date relevance are meaningful guest decision factors. The evidence does not include full mobile scroll coverage, opened controls, interaction recordings, date picker behavior, or scroll-depth data.

## Evidence Limitations

- Optional business goals, pilot brief, analytics context, and known organizational priorities were not provided.
- Evidence is based on static screenshots and the derived current-state/component/opportunity documentation.
- No downstream booking, ticketing, table reservation, event detail, checkout, opened FAQ, opened chat, opened menu, empty-state, loading-state, or error-state evidence was provided.
- No behavioral analytics, conversion data, support data, scroll-depth data, guest research, package rules, loyalty rules, inventory, pricing, or operational fulfillment data was provided.
- Mobile screenshots do not necessarily prove the absence of controls across the entire mobile experience; they only show that certain controls were not visible in the reviewed captures.

## Recommendation Answer

Of all observed opportunities, the few directions most worth pursuing are:

1. Strengthen the entertainment-to-resort value connection because it has the clearest combined guest, business, and strategic upside across packages, cross-sell, and loyalty relevance.
2. Improve date- and mobile-led entertainment discovery because guests need continuity across timing, category, and long-page navigation, especially where the observed mobile evidence shows less visible discovery support.
3. Increase confidence in commercial and operational decisions because multiple CTA, VIP, support, availability, and fulfillment questions may affect whether guests can act confidently.
4. Connect event choices to venue and premium context because venue and premium information appears important but may be separated from event selection moments.
